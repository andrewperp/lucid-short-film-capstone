About the Project

LUCID is my capstone short film and one of the most meaningful creative projects I have worked on. The film explores the unstable line between dreams and waking life, using suspense, surreal visual moments, and cinematic atmosphere to create a world that feels both immersive and slightly unreal.

I had all of last semester to plan out the script, but the actual process of making the film taught me far more than I expected. More than anything, this capstone pushed me not only to grow as a filmmaker, but to become more creative as a person and think outside the box when solving problems in production and post-production.

Logline

LUCID is a short psychological narrative film that follows the unsettling collapse of certainty between dreams and waking life, blending cinematic beauty with tension, distortion, and emotional unease.

Process

Concept Development

LUCID began as an idea centered on uncertainty, dream logic, and the emotional tension that comes from not fully knowing what is real. From the beginning, I wanted it to feel polished and cinematic rather than purely experimental, with a visual language built around both beauty and unease.

Pre-Production

Even after spending all of last semester planning the script, pre-production taught me how different an idea feels once it starts becoming a real film. This stage involved refining the visual direction, preparing scenes and locations, and thinking through how each moment would support the tone of the story.

Production

Production was where the film truly came alive. It pushed me to adapt constantly, make creative decisions under pressure, and find solutions in real time. What surprised me most was how fun the capstone became once everything started moving from the page into actual performances, camera work, and collaboration.

Post-Production

Post-production was where the identity of LUCID became fully shaped. Editing, sound, pacing, and visual refinement helped transform the raw footage into something more immersive and emotionally cohesive. This stage also showed me how much stronger a film becomes when ideas are challenged, refined, and rebuilt through collaboration.

What I Learned

This project pushed me in ways I did not fully expect. It challenged me to trust my visual instincts, collaborate more openly, and solve problems creatively when things changed during production and post. More than anything, LUCID reminded me that filmmaking is not just about technical skill. It is about staying open, adapting quickly, and letting a project evolve into something stronger than what you first imagined.
